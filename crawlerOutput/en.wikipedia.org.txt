<p>The <b>71st British Academy Film Awards</b>, more commonly known as the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Film_Awards" title="British Academy Film Awards">BAFTAs</a>, were held on 18 February 2018 at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> in London, to honour the best national and foreign films of 2017. Presented by the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_of_Film_and_Television_Arts" title="British Academy of Film and Television Arts">British Academy of Film and Television Arts</a> (BAFTA), accolades were given for the best feature-length film and documentaries from any nation screened at British cinemas in 2017. Following revelations of sexual harassment by men in the film industry, many attendees wore black or a badge to show their support for the <a href="/wiki/Time%27s_Up_(movement)" title="Time's Up (movement)">"Time's Up" movement</a>.</p>
<p>The nominees were announced on 9 January 2018 by <a href="/wiki/Letitia_Wright" title="Letitia Wright">Letitia Wright</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Dormer" title="Natalie Dormer">Natalie Dormer</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shape_of_Water_(film)" title="The Shape of Water (film)">The Shape of Water</a></i> had the most nominations of any film, with twelve; <i><a href="/wiki/Darkest_Hour_(film)" title="Darkest Hour (film)">Darkest Hour</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Three_Billboards_Outside_Ebbing,_Missouri" title="Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri">Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri</a></i> followed with nine nominations each. As with the <a href="/wiki/75th_Golden_Globe_Awards" title="75th Golden Globe Awards">75th Golden Globe Awards</a>, there was criticism for the lack of female nominees in the best director category, with <a href="/wiki/Greta_Gerwig" title="Greta Gerwig">Greta Gerwig</a> notably not nominated for <i><a href="/wiki/Lady_Bird_(film)" title="Lady Bird (film)">Lady Bird</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri</i>, directed by Martin McDonagh, won five awards including best film and best British film; <a href="/wiki/Frances_McDormand" title="Frances McDormand">Frances McDormand</a> won the best actress award for her performance in the film. <a href="/wiki/Gary_Oldman" title="Gary Oldman">Gary Oldman</a> won the best actor award for his portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> in <i>Darkest Hour</i>. <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_del_Toro" title="Guillermo del Toro">Guillermo del Toro</a> won best director award for <i>The Shape of Water</i>, one of three awards for the film. In the supporting-actor categories, <a href="/wiki/Allison_Janney" title="Allison Janney">Allison Janney</a> won Best supporting actress for her role in <i><a href="/wiki/I,_Tonya" title="I, Tonya">I, Tonya</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Sam_Rockwell" title="Sam Rockwell">Sam Rockwell</a> won best supporting actor for his role in <i>Three Billboards</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-‚Äúvariety‚Äù_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-‚Äúvariety‚Äù-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Roger_Deakins" title="Roger Deakins">Roger Deakins</a> won his fourth BAFTA for best cinematography for <i><a href="/wiki/Blade_Runner_2049" title="Blade Runner 2049">Blade Runner 2049</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Sir Ridley Scott</a> was awarded the BAFTA fellowship for contribution to cinema, presented by the <a href="/wiki/Prince_William,_Duke_of_Cambridge" title="Prince William, Duke of Cambridge">Duke of Cambridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-‚Äúvariety‚Äù_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-‚Äúvariety‚Äù-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ceremony">Ceremony</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=71st_British_Academy_Film_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ceremony">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ceremony was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> at 9 p.m. <a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a>, around an hour later than the actual ceremony. <a href="/wiki/Joanna_Lumley" title="Joanna Lumley">Joanna Lumley</a> presented the awards for the first time, succeeding <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Fry" title="Stephen Fry">Stephen Fry</a> who had previously hosted the ceremony twelve times.<sup id="cite_ref-Lumley_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lumley-1">[1]</a></sup> Lumley was the first female host since <a href="/wiki/Mariella_Frostrup" title="Mariella Frostrup">Mariella Frostrup</a> co-presented with Fry in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-Lumley_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lumley-1">[1]</a></sup> The ceremony remained at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> for a second consecutive year. Following revelations of sexual harassment by men in the film industry, many attendees wore black or a badge to show their support for the <a href="/wiki/Time%27s_Up_(movement)" title="Time's Up (movement)">"Time's Up" movement</a>. In addition, several brought activists as their partner to the ceremony.<sup id="cite_ref-‚Äúvariety‚Äù_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-‚Äúvariety‚Äù-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ceremony began with a dance segment by <a href="/wiki/Cirque_du_Soleil" title="Cirque du Soleil">Cirque du Soleil</a> in homage to <i>The Shape of Water</i>. Cellist <a href="/wiki/Sheku_Kanneh-Mason" title="Sheku Kanneh-Mason">Sheku Kanneh-Mason</a> performed for the second consecutive year during the in Memoriam section. He, along with some of his siblings, played the Hebrew love song "Evening of Roses" ("Erev Shel Shoshanim") during the segment.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Those remembered included <a href="/wiki/Bill_Paxton" title="Bill Paxton">Bill Paxton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Shepard" title="Sam Shepard">Sam Shepard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Moore" title="Roger Moore">Roger Moore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jill_Messick" title="Jill Messick">Jill Messick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Allison_Shearmur" title="Allison Shearmur">Allison Shearmur</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
